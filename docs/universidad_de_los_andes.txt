The Universidad de los Andes (Uniandes) is a private university founded in 1948 in Bogotá, Colombia, by Mario Laserna Pinzón, with a vision for a secular, humanist, and independent higher education institution inspired by the U.S. university model. It also has a campus in Cartagena. Uniandes is highly regarded, consistently ranking among the top universities in Latin America and as the best in Colombia by various international classifications like QS World University Rankings. It received High Quality Institutional Accreditation for 10 years in 2015, being the first private university in Colombia to achieve the maximum accreditation period. The university boasts 731 faculty members, 73% of whom hold doctorates, and offers 44 undergraduate, 87 master's, and 17 doctoral programs across 12 academic units. Its campus in central Bogotá has grown significantly, incorporating both existing industrial structures and new constructions, totaling over 172,000 square meters. Uniandes is also a prominent research institution with 146 recognized research groups and a strong focus on international collaborations and academic exchanges. Its symbols include "El Bobo" (a statue of San Alberto Magno) and "Séneca" the goat mascot.
