The Pontificia Universidad Javeriana (PUJ), a private Catholic institution, was established in 1623 and has campuses in Bogotá and Cali, Colombia. It has been recognized as the number one university in Colombia by Times Higher Education (2018-2022) and ranked 13th in Iberoamerica by QS in 2023. The PUJ was the first Colombian university to receive High Quality Institutional Accreditation from the CNA. Founded and governed by the Society of Jesus, its mission emphasizes integral formation, critical thinking, and social responsibility, inspired by Gospel values. The university offers a wide range of academic programs, with notable strengths in Medicine and Economic and Administrative Sciences. Its history spans two periods: the Viceregal era (1623-1767) and its re-establishment in 1930. The Bogotá campus features 18 hectares with 46 buildings, including the Hospital Universitario San Ignacio, while the Cali campus is located in a rural setting in Pance. The university's libraries boast extensive collections, including rare books, and the Bogotá library offers 24-hour service. The PUJ also plays a leading role in global educational initiatives, particularly concerning technology and integral ecology.
