The Universidad de Antioquia (U. de A.) is a Colombian public higher education institution located in the department of Antioquia, founded on February 9, 1801, by authorization of King Charles IV of Spain. It is recognized as the second-best public university in the country, with its Faculty of Medicine being the top-ranked in Colombia. The university is an autonomous entity with a special regime, offering approximately 130 undergraduate programs and 184 postgraduate programs across 14 faculties, 4 schools, 4 institutes, and 3 corporations. It received high-quality institutional accreditation for 10 years in 2012, making it one of the few universities in Colombia to achieve this. Beyond academics and research, the U. de A. is a significant cultural center, housing a university museum, a central library with extensive collections, a university theater, and a cultural radio station. Its research efforts are robust, with over 160 recognized research groups and numerous international publications.
