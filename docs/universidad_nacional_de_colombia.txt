The Universidad Nacional de Colombia (UNAL) is a public, national, and autonomous university founded on September 22, 1867, with a significant historical and academic presence in Latin America. Its main campus, Ciudad Universitaria in Bogotá, is the largest in the country, featuring 17 buildings declared national monuments. UNAL has multiple campuses across Colombia, including Medellín, Manizales, Palmira, and several national presence sites in regions like Amazonia and the Caribbean. With over 57,000 students and nearly 3,000 faculty members (half with doctorates), it is one of Colombia's largest academic institutions. The university offers 97 undergraduate programs, 86 specializations, 40 medical/odontological specialties, 171 master's, and 70 doctoral programs. In 2010, it received High Quality Institutional Accreditation for 10 years across all its campuses and is consistently ranked as the best university in Colombia by various national and international metrics. UNAL is known for its rigorous admission process, with only about 15% of applicants gaining entry. It also has a strong research focus, with numerous laboratories and a free-distribution newspaper, UN Periódico, which disseminates its research and cultural content nationally and internationally.
